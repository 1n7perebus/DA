ANALYTICAL PSYCHOLOGY

Analytical psychology, created by Carl Jung, delves into the hidden parts of the mind to grasp how people behave and form personalities. It focuses on the collective unconscious, a shared pool of symbols and patterns across cultures. Analytical psychology believes in individuals reaching their fullest potential and becoming whole through a process called individuation, where they blend their conscious and unconscious thoughts. Dreams, fantasies, and symbols are seen as tools for understanding oneself better, aiming for more self-awareness and inner balance.
